Regina's Copycat Chipotle Guacamole

Guacamole is one of my favorite foods to eat! It is healthy and oh so delicious whether you are eating it with toast, chips, or in a burrito! I also happen to love Chipotle's version of guacamole so I decided to make my own copycat after doing some research online. Cause let's face it, that stuff's $$$$ and I ain't made of money. So if you also like Chipotle guac and wanna save some moolah, check out this recipe!

Here are the ingredients you will need:

  • 1 large Hass Avocado (DUH!)
  • some parseley (honestly I kind of eyeball this as sometimes I want more or less of it in my guac)
  • 1 tbls of lemon or lime juice
  • half a red onion diced
  • a pinch of salt (just add more if it doesnt taste salty enough for ya)
  • a pinch of pepper
  • 1 Jalepeno (I left this out cause I don't like Jalepeno but feel free to chop this up and throw it in)

Follow these steps to create your guacamole:

  1. First, you want to prep your ingredients. Start with cutting open your avocados and scoop out the goods into a bowl.
  2. Add in your salt, pepper and citrus fluids
  3. Dice up your red onion and toss those in too with the parsely
  4. If you are fancy and have a pestle and motar, use that to mush up the avocado with the other ingredients. If you don't, fear not! A fork or a potato masher works just fine (I would use the potato masher if you are making a bigger serving of this)
  5. Don't over mix your guac unless you want it extra smooth. I prefer it on the chunkier side
